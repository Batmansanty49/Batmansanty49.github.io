All about 6G Network

The sixth generation of mobile networks, commonly known as 6G, is currently under development. It promises to drastically outperform 5G networks in terms of speed, latency, and capacity. While 5G is built primarily for connecting people and things, 6G is expected to bridge the gap between the physical, digital, and human worlds.

Key expected features include terahertz (THz) frequency utilization, artificial intelligence (AI) natively integrated into the network architecture, and advanced edge computing. This will enable applications such as deeply immersive extended reality (XR), high-fidelity holographic communications, and autonomous systems operating with zero-latency synchronization.

All about SRv6

Segment Routing over IPv6 (SRv6) is an advanced routing architecture designed to simplify network programming and operation. Unlike traditional routing protocols that rely heavily on complex state maintenance across the network, SRv6 encodes the routing path directly into the packet header as a list of segments.

By leveraging the existing IPv6 architecture, SRv6 eliminates the need for protocols like MPLS or LDP. It offers unparalleled programmability, allowing network engineers to define precise paths for traffic engineering, service chaining, and VPNs with incredible efficiency. It represents a major leap towards more scalable and automated infrastructure.
